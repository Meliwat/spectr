Pure Black

VSCO is a no-chrome photo editor: a pure-black canvas (#000000) where the photograph is the only color. UI is monochrome white-on-black, hairline-thin sliders, and uppercase Inter labels with wide tracking. The film-preset carousel and the adjust-tool tray are the whole interface — nothing competes with the image.

Dark Mode Notes

VSCO is dark-native — the editor is always pure black (#000000), there is no light theme for the editing surface. The philosophy: the photograph is the only source of color, so every chrome element is desaturated white (#FFFFFF) or one of three grays. Sliders are 1px hairlines on a #3A3A3A track with a solid white knob. Labels are uppercase Inter with +1.6px tracking. Feed and profile screens use a near-black #0E0E0E surface; the image grid never dims. Accent color does not exist — selection is communicated by a 2px white ring, never a tint.
