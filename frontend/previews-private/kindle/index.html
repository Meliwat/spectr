The Lighthouse at Dawn

The sea had been quiet for three days, and Mara had learned not to trust the quiet. She climbed the spiral stair before first light, counting the steps the way her father had taught her, each one worn soft by a century of keepers.

At the top the lamp still turned, patient and enormous, throwing its long arm of light across water the color of slate. She rested her palm on the brass and felt the faint warmth that never quite left it.

Below, the village was a scatter of dark roofs. Somewhere down there a boat was already missing, though no one knew it yet but her.

Dark Mode Notes

Kindle's reading surface is theme-driven, not OS-driven: the reader honors the user's chosen page theme (White / Sepia / Green / Dark #2A2A2A / Black #000000) independent of system appearance. App chrome (Library, Home, settings) does follow system dark: canvas #0E0E0E, surfaces lift to #1A1A1A. Amazon Orange #FF9900 is identical across themes — it is the progress and CTA signal everywhere. On a true-black reader theme, body ink is a dimmed #C8C8C8 (never pure white) to reduce halation in the dark. Cover art always stays full-color; only chrome dims.
